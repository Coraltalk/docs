This guide helps students join classes, complete oral assignments, and use Coraltalk's learning features.

## Join a class

<Steps>
  <Step title="Get your class link or code">
    Your teacher will share a **class link** or **class code** via email, LMS, or during class
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Create your account">
    If you don't have an account yet:
    - Go to [coraltalk.com](https://coraltalk.com) and click **Sign up**
    - Choose **I'm a student**
    - Sign up with email or Google
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Join the class">
    - Click the class link, OR
    - Enter the class code on your home page
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Start learning">
    You'll see your class dashboard with available assignments and the 24/7 teaching assistant
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
If your school uses Canvas, you may launch Coraltalk directly from your Canvas course — no separate login needed after the first time.
</Tip>

## Your class dashboard

After joining, your class shows:

| Section | What you'll find |
|---------|------------------|
| **Oral Learning** | Oral assignments from your teacher |
| **Roleplay** | Scenario-based speaking practice |
| **Vid Tickets** | Video prompts and peer responses |
| **Chat with Coral** | 24/7 teaching assistant for questions |
| **Materials** | Course syllabus and resources |

## Chat with your teaching assistant (Coral)

<Accordion title="What is Coral?">
  Coral is your AI teaching assistant trained on your course materials. Ask questions anytime, and Coral will help based on your syllabus and what your teacher uploaded.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="How to use Coral">
  1. Click **Chat with Coral** in your class
  2. Type or speak your question
  3. Get instant answers grounded in course content
  4. Continue the conversation for clarification
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What Coral can help with">
  - Explaining concepts from your syllabus
  - Reviewing material before exams
  - Clarifying assignment instructions
  - Practice questions and study help
</Accordion>

<Warning>
Coral is designed to help you learn, not complete assignments for you. Your teacher can see conversation summaries.
</Warning>

## Complete oral assignments

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the assignment">
    Click on an oral assignment from **Oral Learning**
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Allow microphone access">
    Your browser will ask for microphone permission — click **Allow**
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Have the conversation">
    - Coral will ask you questions based on the assignment
    - Speak naturally and clearly
    - Answer follow-up questions as they come
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Submit">
    When finished, submit your oral assignment
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Wait for evaluation">
    Coral will evaluate your responses (may take several minutes)
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="View feedback">
    Check your grade and feedback when ready (visibility depends on teacher settings)
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
**Testing your microphone**: Click the microphone icon before starting to test your audio. Speak clearly and in a quiet environment for best results.
</Tip>

## Complete roleplay assignments

Roleplay assignments put you in realistic scenarios:

1. Open the roleplay assignment
2. Read the scenario and your role
3. Have a natural conversation with the AI character
4. Complete the objectives given in the scenario
5. Submit and wait for evaluation

**Examples**: 
- Practice a job interview
- Negotiate as a business professional
- Have a conversation in a foreign language
- Play a historical figure in a debate

## Participate in Vid Tickets

Vid tickets let you record video responses and engage with classmates:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Record your response">
    Watch the prompt and record your video answer
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Unlock peer videos">
    After submitting, you can view and comment on classmate responses
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Engage in dialogue">
    Reply to peers, continue discussions, and learn from different perspectives
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Technical requirements

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Browser">
    **Recommended browsers**:
    - Chrome (latest version)
    - Safari (latest version)
    - Edge (latest version)
    - Firefox (latest version)
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Microphone">
    - Built-in laptop microphone works fine
    - Headset microphone recommended for better quality
    - Check privacy settings to allow browser microphone access
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Internet">
    - Stable internet connection required
    - Minimum 5 Mbps download/upload
    - Use WiFi or ethernet when possible
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Tips for success

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Test your setup first">
    Before your first assignment, test your microphone and internet connection in a low-stakes conversation with Coral.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Find a quiet space">
    Background noise can affect transcription quality. Find a quiet room for oral assignments.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Speak clearly and naturally">
    Don't rush. Speak as if you're explaining to a classmate. Pauses are okay.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Use Coral for practice">
    Before an oral exam, practice explaining concepts to Coral in the regular chat. It's great preparation.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Check deadlines">
    Assignments may have due dates. Check your class dashboard regularly.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Troubleshooting

<Accordion title="Microphone not working">
  1. Check browser permissions (look for microphone icon in address bar)
  2. Make sure no other app is using your microphone
  3. Try refreshing the page
  4. See [Common issues](/troubleshooting/common-issues)
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Can't hear Coral">
  1. Check your volume settings
  2. Make sure your browser isn't muted
  3. Try using headphones
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Class link isn't working">
  1. Make sure you're logged in
  2. Try copying and pasting the full URL
  3. Ask your teacher for a new link
  4. Contact support@coraltalk.com
</Accordion>
